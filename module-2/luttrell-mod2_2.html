All About The Luttrells

My name is Jason Luttrell. My last name has noble origins that traces its lineage from the Norman kingdoms of norther France, through the battle of Hastings in the eleventh-century. As part of the conquering Norman invasion my ancestors became British nobility and maintained their noble status for hundreds of years. Two brothers in the beginning of the eighteenth-century fled England to escape scandal and ’ prison to the shores of America. These two brothers are the progenitors of all the Luttrells in the United States.

When the two fallen nobles arrived in the United States, they traveled to present day North Carolina and bought a truly massive swath of land that included most of western North Carolina, part of Tennessee and part of Kentucky. They were incredibly wealthy again with such a massive swath of land, until the revolutionary war. When the colonial army won independence, the confederated states confiscated the land of many land owners to redistribute it to the soldiers who fought in the war.

While the two brothers still retained a very large estate, measurable in square miles, they had lost most of what they had. But they still did quite well, and their ancestors still occupy North Carolina and Tennessee today. There was also a rather large group of Luttrells that traveled to Southern Illinois in search of land. That group is where I am descended from.

The Luttrells of my line (the Illinois Luttrells) are marked by a profound devotion to Christ, an extremely strong work ethic, and a connection to nature. Of course there are some exceptions, but not many. In fact, one of my Aunts was a miracle worker. She was known to be able to stop the bleeding of any wound, simply by praying. The effect of her prayers was immediate, and even the town doctor would send word to Aunt Blackie when someone was injured to stop the bleeding. It worked on livestock and people.
